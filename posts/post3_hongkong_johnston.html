Short but adventurous hike in Hong Kong

Hong Kong, Mount Johnston – 香港，玉桂山

24 Feb 2023

Traveling time from Central Station ~30min
Hiking Distance 4km
Elevation Gain +300m
Difficulty Difficult

In late February, I had the chance to escape the cold of Shanghai for the nice spring weather of Hong Kong during my company’s annual team building. Always happy to spend more time in this amazing city, I decided to stay over the weekend and explore a few of the great trails that Hong Kong had to offer.

On Friday mid-afternoon, after finishing all my meetings, I headed with one of my coworker towards Mount Johnston for what we thought would be a nice little stroll by the sea. Little did we know that this trail was actually quite a serious one with some pretty technical parts, for our greatest pleasure!

After coming out of the subway station, it took us some time to find the start of the hike, which was hidden in between two tiny yellow buildings, behind a fence that we had to climb over. Thankfully, a friendly street sweeper was taking his break there and showed us the right access point.

Once on the path, it was all the way up towards the summit. The climb was not long, but quite strenuous with a very steep slope. Some parts had ropes installed to help going up, even though it was not always necessary. There were few forks on the way, but as long as we were going up, we could not get lost. After about 20min of constant uphill, we reached the top and were rewarded by fantastic views of Lama Island and the South China Sea.

When continuing further on the ridge, we finally caught our first glimpse of Ap Lei Pai, a small island connected to Mount Johnston by a thin sandbar, also known as tombolo. The landscape was stunning with the blue sky, several boats cruising around and birds flying close to us. We stopped for a while to take some pictures, breath the fresh air and just enjoy this magical environment.

After that, the scary way downhill started towards the sandbar. It was very rocky and slippery. We slowly descended, trying our best to keep our balance and not to fall. We did not have proper hiking shoes and were carrying our full backpack with work computer and traveling clothes. It was definitely not the best idea in the world, but sometimes, you just have to do with what you have.

After making it safely to the tombolo, we enjoyed a nice break and dipped our foot in the water. My coworker went searching for some seashells to bring back to his daughter, while I was enjoying the refreshing seawater. The atmosphere was peaceful, even though a few trashes were scattered over the little beach, brought there by the ocean.

The trail was continuing further on Ap Lei Pai and it was supposed to have a very unique tidal pool at the end. However, the sun was already starting to be quite low over the horizon, so we decided to head back. We had two options: to take the same path uphill or go around Mount Johnston, towards the left when facing it from the sandbar. We chose the second alternative.

From there, the serious things started! The alternative path was mainly on rocks and involved lots of scrambling, ladders climbing and walking on thin edges above vertical drops leading directly to the sea or stones at the bottom. It definitely required a bit of skills to get through it. However, several planks, ropes and anchors were installed to make things easier. The scenery was absolutely breathtaking and we frequently stopped to admire it.

On the way, we found a rusted chair facing the ocean. It seemed to have been posted there for years. Of course, I could not resist the wish to go sit on it and get a great shot. We also met two guys practicing rock climbing on one of the stonewalls with the sunset in the background. Definitely not a bad spot!

After all these thrilling adventures, without much transition, we reached again the city and were greeted by a few high-rise buildings. From there, we just had to walk a little over one kilometer to reach the nearest subway station and go in search of our delicious dinner.

Overall, it was a very short hike, but it took us more than two hours to complete it, with all the breaks we had, just to take it all in and appreciate the moment.

Pros

I highly recommend this hike for anyone who just has a few hours to spend hiking and can’t dedicate a full day to it. It is very easily accessible by subway or by bus and can be completed in less than two hours.

In addition, the scenery is absolutely stunning and quite unique with this beautiful tombolo connecting the tiny island of Ap Lei Pai.

Finally, this trail is very fun if you are an experienced hiker and enjoy a bit of a challenge.

Cons

As mentioned above, this path is quite technical. It requires a little bit of skills to get through certain parts and can be difficult for anyone not used to hiking in difficult terrains.

If you are scared of height, it is not recommended to go around Mount Johnston on the way back, but rather to use the same trail to return.
